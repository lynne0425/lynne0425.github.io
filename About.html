What is Happening?

  • women comprise 47% of the total U.S. workforce and make up to 85% of consumer purchases, they only hold 25% of professional tech occupations
  • women are usually far outnumbered
  • 20% of women remain in junior positions even when they are older than 35, which means that they are “3.5x more likely to be in junior positions” than men of the same age
  • Spending money on panels and ads do not make up for a lack of internal inclusion initiatives. not enough to just hire more women, companies need to put in the effort to make their cultures inclusive from the ground up

Current Solutions

  • diversity holds the key to true performance excellence in a company
    • study from 2011 in Standard & Poor’s Composite 1500 list found that female representation in executive management positions led to an increase of $42 million in firm value.
  • encouraged by the collaboration of the over 200 organizations committed to President Obama’s Computer Science for All
  • partnership with others, created the Tech Opportunity Fund to increase educational access and support diversity in the ever-growing world of technology.
  • All demographics should be represented
  • women to be able to achieve leadership roles in this industry
