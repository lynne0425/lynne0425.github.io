Edith Clarke (1883-1959)
  • Invented the Clarke Calculator
  • Inventors Hall of Fame, joining the ranks with Thomas Edison and Nikola Tesla for her Clarke Calculator
  • The first woman in electrical engineering
  • The first woman voted into the American Institute of Electrical Engineer(AIEE)

Katherine Johnson (1918-)
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • One of three Afican American students hand picked to be the first black students to attend the University of West Virginia
  • John Glenn, before his flight called upon Johnson to run the calculations once more before he took off-- “If she says they’re good, then I’m ready to go.”
  • First woman in the Flight Research Division to receive credit as an author of a research report.

Ellen Ochoa (1958-)
  • Studied electrical engineering at Stanford University
  • Worked as a specialist in the development of optical systems at NASA
  • First Hispanic Astronaut
  • She worked with different satellites, studying the Earth’s atmosphere and the Sun’s interactions with it
